About

Formerly:
bartender, international cabin crew, and interior designer.

Currently:
Dance teacher, free spirit, and digital artisan.

I'm originally from Connecticut but have also held addresses in Seville, Spain, and Dubai, UAE. I’m formally Boston-educated in Communications, Spanish, and Architectural History at Northeastern University, with a certificate in Residential Interior Design from the New England Institute of Art. I grew up wanting to design buildings, and after a few crucial diversions, I’m now a mainly self-taught architect of interfaces for web and mobile.

I use my cross-disciplinary design background and customer service expertise to create effortless and engaging experiences for both my clients’ audiences as well as for my clients themselves.

I have lived all over the world and have mastered the art of Google Hangouts, the science of time zone management and the world of remote work. For the right projects, I’m up for working with clients all over the globe.


Fun facts & Obsessions:

My extracurricular activities include guitar, boxing, latin dancing, and snowboarding. I was a featured dancer in Martha Wash’s music video for her ballad, “I’ve Got You.” I’m of Italian descent, but I speak Spanish and have a Yorkie named Chico. I don’t own a TV, and my favorite food is coffee.
